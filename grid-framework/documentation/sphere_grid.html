Spherical grids

The GFSphericalGrid class

Spherical grids allow navigation in a spherical space using one distance and two angles. The grid itself is defined by the radius of the first sphere, the number of meridians spanning from one pole of the sphere to the other and the number of parallel circles running orthogonal to the meridians. Successive spheres enclose preceding spheres.

Geographic coordinates are the most familiar instance of this type of grid. Just like a planet a spherical grid has a north- and south pole, an equator, a primary meridian and a rotation axis going through the two poles. The north pole is in positive Y-direction, the south pole in negative Y-direction. The primary meridian in in positive Z-direction.

Coordinate systems of spherical grids

There are three coordinate systems supported: spherical, geographic and grid, all angles given in radians.

Spherical coordinate system

There are three components: radius, polar angle θ and azimuth angle φ, in that order. The radius of a point is simply the distance from the origin of the grid. The polar angle is the angle between the position vector of the point and the polar axis of the grid, positive on the northern hemisphere and negaive on the southern one. The azimuth angle is the angle between the vector and the primary meridian, counter-clockwise when the polar axis if facing towards the observer.

The azimuth is somewhat special: opposite of the primary meridian it is π (180°), as one would expect. If we rotate further the angle keeps growing instead of shrinking or turning negative. In other words, the azimuth angle works the same as the angle in coordinate in a polar coordinate system.

Azimuth and polar angle are undefined at the origin, at the poles the azimuth is undefined.

Grid coordinate system

This is the same as the spherical excpet that the coordinate are not absulte values but rather relative to the properties of the grid. This means the first value is the sphere, the second is the parallel and the third one is the meridian. Other than that everything is the same as for spherical coordinates.

Geographic coordinate system

This coordinate system is used for navigation on Earth. Every point is given by an altitude, latitude and longitude. The altitude is the distance from the surface of the first sphere, not the distance from the origin. The altitude can be negative if the point is beneath the surface.

Latitude and longitude work as usual, the norhtern hemisphere has positive latitude and the eastern hemisphere has positive longitude. The sign of the meridian opposite of the primary meridian is undefined.
